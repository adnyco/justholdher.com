The Story

Not one spiral notebook. Several.

Updated every two hours.

A hand-drawn grid.
Diapers. Readings. Injections. Feedings.

Then the notes in the margins. The things the grid could not hold. A body changing. A pattern breaking. A question that would not go away.

What began as a record of care became a witness to everything medicine could measure… and everything it could not.

This is the world of Just Hold Her: one child, one house, one notebook, and the long discipline of beginning again.

The Book

Just Hold Her is drawn from Judy Wright’s experience caring for a foster child whose life was never expected to last.

This is not the story of a cure.
It is the story of what happens when love arrives before certainty.
When a mother’s instinct collides with prognosis, protocol, and time.
When the smallest changes begin to carry the weight of hope.

It was lived in feedings, alarms, handwritten notes, and the long repetition of beginning again.

A memoir of presence under pressure.
Of attachment formed in the shadow of loss.
Of what it costs to stay when no one can promise the ending.

A witness story. Not a rescue story.

What Awaits You in the Book

I · The Record

A life documented in two-hour cycles. The spiral notebook becomes the book’s central artifact and its moral pressure.

II · The Child

A baby the system had already prepared to lose. Four pounds. Twenty-seven diagnoses. No plan beyond placement.

III · The Labor

The daily work of keeping a child alive inside a household. Not abstract care. Physical care. Repeated care. Care that rearranges everything around it.

IV · The Friction

Doctors, protocols, appointments, and institutional decisions colliding with what Judy witnesses firsthand in the child she is holding.

V · The Attachment

Not a story about detachment or noble distance. A story about what happens when love keeps growing inside uncertainty.

VI · The Staying

This is the book’s deepest movement: not rescue, not triumph, but the quiet defiance of remaining present when no one can promise what comes next.
