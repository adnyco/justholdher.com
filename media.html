The Story

“Would you, please… just hold her until she dies?”

That was the call.

The baby was two months old. Four pounds. Blind, deaf, insulin-dependent, and tethered to monitors. She arrived with twenty-seven diagnoses and no plan beyond placement.

They did not ask Judy Wright to save her.

They asked her to stay.

Just Hold Her is the true story of what followed: a long medical vigil inside Michigan’s foster care system, reconstructed from the spiral notebooks Judy kept in real time as she tracked feedings, glucose readings, injections, alarms, and the changing patterns of a medically fragile child’s body.

At the center of the memoir is one persistent question:

What about breast milk?

What begins as emergency foster care becomes a sustained witness to the collision of maternal attention, medical authority, and institutional decision-making.

This is not a rescue story.
It is a witness story.

Key Facts for Press

The Document

Spiral notebooks kept in real time

The memoir is built from spiral notebooks containing hand-drawn grids of feedings, glucose readings, insulin doses, oxygen data, and care notes recorded across 674 days. The notebook is not a retrospective device. It is the book’s primary documentary source and central visual motif.

“It was noted in the nomination form that for this one child they attended 495 medical appointments in an 18-month time period.”— Chuck Ludwig, Director of CARE House

The Placement

Michigan foster care, high medical acuity

The child arrived with twenty-seven diagnoses, including congenital syphilis, insulin dependence, blindness, deafness, and failure to thrive. The medical system had prepared for death. There was no plan beyond placement.

The Conflict

A mother’s witness versus protocol

At the center of the book is a practical medical question Judy refused to stop asking: “What about breast milk?” That question brought her into repeated conflict with institutional authority and became one of the memoir’s defining tensions.

The Recognition

Michigan Supreme Court, 2012

Judy and John Wright were named Michigan’s Foster Parents of the Year after twelve years and fifty-five foster placements, many of them medically fragile.

The Legacy

John and Judy Wright Scholarship

Their years of foster care and advocacy later inspired the John & Judy Wright Scholarship for Foster Children, created with the Ennis Center for Children to support foster youth pursuing higher education.

About the Author

Judy Wright

Judy Wright is, above all else, a mother.

Alongside her late husband, John — a Vietnam veteran and corporate pilot — she built a household shaped by the daily work of caring for children. After raising four children of their own, Judy and John entered Michigan’s foster care system, where over twelve years they fostered fifty-five children, many of them medically fragile.

Their home became a place of readiness: bassinets brought up in the night, appointments folded into the week, and children held without guarantees. That work made Judy an advocate when she needed to be, and a writer only because some things she witnessed demanded a record.

In 2012, Judy and John were recognized as Oakland County Foster Parents of the Year and Michigan Foster Parents of the Year. Their legacy also includes the John & Judy Wright Scholarship for Foster Children, created with the Ennis Center for Children.

Just Hold Her is Judy Wright’s debut memoir, drawn from the spiral notebooks she kept during a two-year medical vigil inside Michigan’s foster care system.
