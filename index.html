Just Hold Her

A memoir of foster care, medicine, and the long nights in between.

“WOULD YOU, PLEASE… JUST HOLD HER UNTIL SHE DIES?”

That was the call.

Two months old. Four pounds.
We named her Jayden.

She arrived swaddled in hospital linens,
tethered to tubes and monitors.
Blind. Deaf. Insulin-dependent.
Hands and feet raw since birth.
Thirty diagnoses. No plan.

They didn't ask us to save her.
They asked us to stay.

What followed wasn't heroic. It was relentless.
Alarms in the dark. A thin-gauge lancet.
A cooler on the porch.
A spiral notebook filling with numbers.

And one question I kept asking, again and again:
What about breast milk?

Just Hold Her is the true story of a foster mother's nightwatch. A literary memoir where medicine, bureaucracy, and human attachment collide, and where faith arrives less as certainty than as presence.

The story stays with Jayden from that first call into the long months that followed, bearing witness to the quiet, aching grace at the edge of life. Where protocol ends, and staying asks for everything you have.

About the Author

JUDY WRIGHT

Judy Wright is a foster mother, caregiver, and advocate whose life has been shaped by the demanding, ordinary work of staying. Alongside her late husband, John, she fostered fifty-five children over twelve years, many medically fragile.

In 2012, Judy and John were recognized as Oakland County Foster Parents of the Year by the Oakland County Board of Commissioners and named Michigan's Foster Parents of the Year by the Michigan Supreme Court. Their years of hard work and dedication helped inspire the John and Judy Wright Scholarship for Foster Children in collaboration with the Ennis Center for Children.

Just Hold Her is Judy's debut memoir, drawn from the spiral notebooks she kept during a two-year nightwatch that began with a single call to “just hold” a dying baby and became a sustained act of care, witness, and love without guarantees.

Judy writes for foster, medical, and faith-aware communities about presence, caregiving, and the courage required to remain available when certainty is gone.

About the Book

Just Hold Her

When the call comes to “just hold her until she dies,” a foster mother opens her arms to a child the world is already turning away from.

Just Hold Her is the true story of a long vigil lived inside ordinary days. A quiet house becomes a lifeline, where medicine, bureaucracy, and human attachment collide, and where care is measured in alarms, feedings, phone calls, and the choice to show up again. Built from the spiral notebook Judy kept through those nights, the memoir traces an emotional arc from fear to fierce attachment, and the cost of staying without guarantees.

A memoir of presence over perfection, Just Hold Her speaks to anyone who has carried responsibility without certainty, loved without promise, or learned that hope can arrive quietly, one hour at a time.

For caregivers, foster parents, medical staff, and anyone who has lived at the edge of what medicine can do, this is a story of witness, compassion, and shared humanity.
